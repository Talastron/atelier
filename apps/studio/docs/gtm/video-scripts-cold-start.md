# 5 Cold-Start Video Scripts (Track 2)

_Hook-engineered for first-3-second retention — the version that earns algorithmic reach from a zero-follower account. Pairs with the [outreach kit](./outreach-kit-first-cohort.md) (Track 1 feeds these with real Style DNA cards)._

## How to use these

- **The first 2 seconds are 90% of the result.** A zero-follower account gets reach only when retention beats the average. Lead with the hook — never a logo, never "hi guys," never slow setup.
- **Visual hook + verbal hook fire together.** Say the bold line *while* something moves on screen (the colour wheel filling, a card appearing).
- **Vertical, captions burned in, shot on a phone.** Polished-but-handmade beats over-produced. This is a person, not a brand ad.
- **Soft CTA only.** "It's called Atelier, link in bio." Never "sign up now."
- **Each script has 2–3 hook variants — test them.** Same body, different first line. The hook is the only variable worth A/B-ing early. Post the same idea with different hooks a few days apart.
- **Comment-bait beats a hard CTA** for reach. End on a question that makes people reply ("what's your dominant colour?"). Replies = the algorithm's strongest signal.
- **Repurpose every one** as a Pinterest Idea Pin + embed in the Journal.

## Script 1 — The Style DNA reveal (the flagship)

**Angle:** the novel, visual "oh that's *me*" payoff. This is your highest-ceiling video. Make it first.

**Hook variants (pick/test):**
- A: *"I fed an AI my entire wardrobe and it told me my 'style DNA' — and it's weirdly accurate."*
- B: *"This is what your wardrobe actually looks like — and most people are shocked by their own."*
- C: *"I photographed every item I own. Here's the colour I didn't realise I was obsessed with."*

**Shot list / VO:**
1. **(0–2s) HOOK** — talk to camera, then immediately cut to screen. Open with the line over a fast screen-record of the **colour wheel filling in**.
2. **(2–6s)** *"You add your clothes — just phone photos — and it builds this."* Show the wheel + treemap rendering. Let the visual breathe; don't over-talk.
3. **(6–12s)** *"Turns out 38% of my wardrobe is one colour. I had no idea I was that predictable."* Point at the dominant slice. Self-deprecating = relatable.
4. **(12–18s)** Reveal the **Style DNA share card** sliding out. *"It even makes this — your whole taste on one card."*
5. **(18–22s) CTA + comment bait** — *"It's called Atelier. What do you think your dominant colour is? I bet you're wrong."*

**On-screen text:** "YOUR STYLE DNA" → "38% one colour 😳" → "the whole thing on one card"

**Why it works:** novelty (nobody's seen their wardrobe as data) + self-recognition (it's about *them*) + a curiosity gap they can only close by trying it. The "I bet you're wrong" ending farms replies.

## Script 2 — The Manifesto reaction

**Angle:** emotional payoff. The AI writing a flattering, accurate read of your taste is the single most screenshot-worthy moment in the product.

**Hook variants:**
- A: *"An AI just wrote a three-paragraph manifesto of my personal style and I had to sit down."*
- B: *"I didn't expect an app to understand my taste better than I do."*
- C: *"Read this out loud and tell me it doesn't sound like a magazine wrote it about you."*

**Shot list / VO:**
1. **(0–3s) HOOK** — face to camera, genuine reaction energy. The line lands, then →
2. **(3–6s)** *"You wear your clothes, log what you actually reach for, and after a while it writes… this."* Cut to the **Manifesto** on screen.
3. **(6–16s)** **Read the manifesto aloud** over slow b-roll of you/your wardrobe. Let the writing do the work — it's good; don't talk over it. Pause on the best line.
4. **(16–20s)** *"It's reading my taste back from what I *actually* wear, not what I say I like."*
5. **(20–24s) CTA + bait** — *"It's called Atelier. Would yours be flattering or brutal? Genuinely asking."*

**On-screen text:** pull the single best sentence of the manifesto as a centred quote-card.

**Why it works:** ego + surprise. People share what flatters them. The read feels earned (from real behaviour), which makes it credible, not horoscope-y.

> Note: the Manifesto unlocks at 30 logged wears — film this once you (or a Tier-A friend) have hit that. Until then, lead with Script 1.

## Script 3 — The cost-per-wear gut-punch (smart, not frugal)

**Angle:** the value reframe — but framed as *intelligence and taste*, never penny-pinching. For the affluent ICP, "I'm smart about what I buy" sells; "I'm saving money" repels.

**Hook variants:**
- A: *"The most expensive thing in my wardrobe is also the worst value — and the cheapest is the best. Watch."*
- B: *"I tracked the cost-per-wear of everything I own. The results made me rethink every purchase."*
- C: *"That £400 coat you 'had to have'? Let's do the maths."*

**Shot list / VO:**
1. **(0–3s) HOOK** — hold up / show the expensive piece. Bold claim.
2. **(3–9s)** *"This coat was £400. Worn four times. That's £100 a wear."* Show the CPW number on screen. *"This jumper was £30, worn ninety times — 33p a wear."*
3. **(9–15s)** *"The app tracks this automatically. It quietly tells you which 'investment pieces' are actually dead weight."* Show the leaderboard / worst-value list.
4. **(15–20s)** *"It changed what I buy. Now I ask: will I wear this a hundred times?"*
5. **(20–24s) CTA + bait** — *"It's called Atelier. What's your best cost-per-wear piece? Mine's a £12 t-shirt, embarrassingly."*

**On-screen text:** "£400 ÷ 4 = £100/wear" → "£30 ÷ 90 = £0.33/wear" → "which is the *real* luxury?"

**Why it works:** a counter-intuitive flip (expensive = bad value) is inherently watchable, and the reframe gives the viewer a smarter identity, not a cheaper one.

## Script 4 — "Nothing to wear" with a full closet (POV)

**Angle:** the universal pain, named sharply. Pure relatability → high saves and shares ("this is so me").

**Hook variants:**
- A: *"You don't have 'nothing to wear.' You have nothing you can *see*. There's a difference."*
- B: *"Why you stand in front of a full wardrobe and feel like you own nothing."*
- C: *"The problem was never that I needed more clothes."*

**Shot list / VO:**
1. **(0–3s) HOOK** — stand in front of the open wardrobe, deliver the line straight to camera.
2. **(3–9s)** *"You own hundreds of things and wear the same eight. Not because you lack clothes — because you can't hold the whole wardrobe in your head."*
3. **(9–15s)** *"So I built it a memory."* Cut to the app — the wardrobe gridded, the Daily Brief composing an outfit from pieces you'd forgotten.
4. **(15–20s)** *"Every morning it just tells me what to wear — from what I already own."*
5. **(20–24s) CTA + bait** — *"It's called Atelier. How many things do you own but never wear? Be honest."*

**On-screen text:** "you don't need MORE" → "you need to SEE it" → "outfits from what you forgot you had"

**Why it works:** it articulates a feeling people have but never named — that's the most shareable kind of content ("finally someone said it").

## Script 5 — Build-in-public / founder story

**Angle:** the underdog. People root for (and follow) a solo founder building something beautiful. This compounds an *audience*, not just views — the people who follow here become your first cohort and word-of-mouth.

**Hook variants:**
- A: *"I'm building a styling app on my own — no funding, no team — and I'm going to show you the whole thing."*
- B: *"Day 1 of getting my wardrobe app its first 100 users with £0 in ads. Follow to watch it work or fail."*
- C: *"Everyone said don't build a consumer app solo. Here's day one anyway."*

**Shot list / VO:**
1. **(0–3s) HOOK** — to camera, honest and a little vulnerable. Stakes stated.
2. **(3–9s)** *"It's called Atelier — it turns the clothes you own into a private archive and reads your personal style."* 2-second product flash (the Style DNA card).
3. **(9–16s)** *"It's live. I have almost no users. So I'm doing it the slow way — one person at a time, in public."*
4. **(16–20s)** *"If you love clothes and want to watch a small thing grow, follow along. I'll show the wins and the flops."*
5. **(20–24s) CTA + bait** — *"What would make you actually try a styling app? Tell me — I'm taking notes."*

**On-screen text:** "solo founder" → "£0 ads" → "first 100 users, in public"

**Why it works:** narrative + stakes + a reason to *follow* (not just watch). The comment bait doubles as genuine product research.

## Posting rhythm (cold start)

- **1 video/day, weekdays.** The first ~20 are reps — your delivery and hooks improve fast. Ship them anyway.
- **Rotate the five angles;** when one outperforms, make three more like it.
- **Re-cut winners** with a new hook a week later — same body, fresh first line.
- **Reply to every comment** in the first hour (the algorithm weighs early engagement heavily, and these are your first prospects).
- **Cross-post** TikTok → Reels → Pinterest Idea Pin → Journal embed. One shoot, four placements.

## The one rule

If a video doesn't earn attention in 2 seconds, nothing downstream matters. Spend your energy on hooks, not editing polish.
