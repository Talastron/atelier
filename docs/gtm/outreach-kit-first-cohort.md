# Hand-to-Hand Outreach Kit — Your First Cohort

_The goal: put real water in the pipe THIS WEEK. Get the first ~10 activated wardrobes and 3 people who post their Style DNA — by hand, not by funnel._
_Companion to [atelier-gtm-strategy.md](./atelier-gtm-strategy.md). This is Track 1 (certain water) from the cold-start plan._

## Mindset

Your first users come from your hands, not an algorithm. This is unscalable on purpose — you're optimising for **feedback, testimonials, and content** (real Style DNA cards), not revenue. Revenue follows. Do ~5–8 personal reach-outs a day; quality over blast.

**This week's targets:**
- 40 personal reach-outs sent
- 10 wardrobes activated (≥15 pieces catalogued + Style DNA generated)
- 3 people who publicly post their Style DNA card
- 5 pieces of usable feedback

---

## Two seeding tiers (pick per person)

**Tier A — Inner circle (give it free).** Closest 10–15 people who fit the taste. Use the `'invited'` rails (free access, no card) so there is **zero friction** — you want their honest reaction and their Style DNA card, not their £79. Friction here costs you feedback.

**Tier B — One degree out (founding offer).** Acquaintances, friends-of-friends, the stylish stranger a friend introduces. Point them to the **14-day trial** + the **Founding Member** price (£79/yr for life, first 100 — code `KYMDE3NG`). Real intent, real validation.

> Rule of thumb: if you'd feel awkward charging them, they're Tier A. If they'd feel flattered to be "let in early," they're Tier B.

---

## Step 1 — Source 40 names (the ICP worksheet)

Don't think "who would use an app." Think **"who, in my life, is visibly serious about clothes?"** Run these prompts and write down every name:

- The friend whose outfits you'd screenshot.
- Anyone who has ever reorganised their wardrobe for fun.
- The colleague/acquaintance always put-together.
- People who already talk about cost-per-wear, capsules, quiet luxury, sustainability-in-fashion.
- Your most stylish family members (sister, cousin, aunt, mother-in-law).
- Friends who travel well / pack beautifully.
- Anyone who's asked you "what should I wear to…"
- One-degree intros: ask 5 close friends "who's the most stylish person you know?" → warm intro.
- People who post outfits / wardrobe content on Instagram (even small accounts).

Aim for 40. Track them (sheet columns below). You'll be surprised how many surface once you stop filtering for "techy."

---

## Step 2 — The reach-out templates

Personal, warm, never salesy. Match the brand voice: understated, editorial, a little exclusive. **Always personalise the first line** — generic = ignored. Send where they actually are (WhatsApp, IG DM, text, email — not LinkedIn).

### Template A — Close friend (Tier A, free)
> Hey [name] — I finally built the thing I've been disappearing into for months. It's called Atelier: you photograph your wardrobe and it becomes this beautiful private archive, then an AI reads your taste and literally writes the *manifesto* of your personal style. It's a bit mad how flattering it is.
>
> You're one of about ten people whose taste I actually trust — can I give you free access and have you try it? I mainly want your honest reaction, and I think you'll genuinely like seeing your "Style DNA." Takes about 10 minutes to set up. Game?

### Template B — Acquaintance / friend-of-a-friend (Tier B, founding)
> Hi [name] — [mutual] mentioned you're someone who really *cares* about clothes, so you're exactly who I made this for. I built Atelier — it turns your own wardrobe into a private archive and writes a read of your personal style (people are oddly obsessed with their "Style DNA" card).
>
> It's just gone live and I'm letting the first hundred people in at the founding price — £79/year, locked for life. There's a 14-day trial first, no commitment. Want me to send you the founding key? I'd love your eyes on it.

### Template C — Warm intro to a stranger (Tier B)
> Hi [name] — [mutual] said you have the best wardrobe of anyone they know, which is the highest compliment for what I'm about to send. I built Atelier, an app that turns the clothes you already own into a beautiful archive and reads your personal style back to you.
>
> No pitch — I'd just genuinely love someone with your eye to try it and tell me what's missing. Happy to set you up with founding access. Could I send a link?

### Template D — The "can you help me" ask (works on almost anyone)
> [name], I need a favour from someone with actual taste. I've built a styling app and I'm terrified it's only obvious to me. Could you spend 10 minutes trying it and telling me the truth? I'll set you up — you might even like your Style DNA card enough to keep it.

## Step 3 — The activation sequence (get them to the "aha")

Reaching out is half. **Activation is the win.** Walk them to value fast:

1. **Hand-hold the setup.** Send: "Add ~15 of your most-worn pieces — phone photos are perfect. Don't overthink it." 15 is the threshold where the Style DNA card sings.
2. **Point them straight at the reveal:** "Once you've added a few, go to **Insights → your Colour profile** and hit **Share my Style DNA**. That's the bit." (← the card you shipped.)
3. **Then nudge the habit:** "Open it tomorrow morning — it'll tell you what to wear. That's the part that sticks."

If someone stalls at cataloguing, offer to add a few pieces *with* them on a call. Unscalable, decisive.

---

## Step 4 — The share ask (turn users into traffic)

This is how Track 1 feeds Track 2. Once someone reacts well:

> "Okay you clearly love it 😄 — would you post your Style DNA card on your [Instagram story / grid] and tag @[atelier handle]? It's the single biggest help you could give me right now. No pressure if it's not your thing."

- Ask **only after** they've expressed delight. Earned, not begged.
- Their post = your first social proof + first cold traffic.
- **Save every Style DNA card** (with permission) → re-feature as Pinterest pins and your own content.

## Handling the usual friction

| They say | You say |
|---|---|
| "I don't have time to add everything." | "Just your 15 most-worn — 10 minutes. You don't need the whole wardrobe to see your Style DNA." |
| "Is it free?" (Tier A) | "For you, completely — I want your honest take, not your money." |
| "What's it cost?" (Tier B) | "£79/year if you're one of the first hundred — locked for life. 14-day trial first, cancel anytime." |
| "I'll do it later." | "Want me to set it up *with* you for 10 minutes this week? I'll screenshare." |
| "I'm not really techy." | "It's just photos of your clothes — if you can use Instagram, you're fine. I'll help." |

## Do / Don't

**Do:** personalise line one · give Tier A genuinely free · hand-hold to the reveal · ask for the share only after delight · capture every testimonial + card · follow up once (not thrice).

**Don't:** mass-blast an identical message · lead with price · ask for the share before they're delighted · chase non-responders more than once · optimise the website instead of doing this (no water = go get water).

---

## Why this works

Premium consumer products are sold by *trusted taste*, not ads. A founding key from a friend who says "your taste is exactly who I built this for" converts at rates no paid channel touches — and each activated user hands you a Style DNA card that becomes the content that starts Track 2. This week is hands. The algorithm comes next.
